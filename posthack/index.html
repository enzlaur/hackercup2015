EXPLAINING THE THEME
.

This year, the United Nations launched 17 new initiatives, called the Sustainable Development Goals, that its member states can use in crafting their agendas and policies in the next 15 years. However, we have a crisis of poor data that hinders data-driven decision-making. There is a need to focus our efforts on building capacity to generate, share and use data in the national and local context.

In line with this year’s Lasallian Mission Week, the Center for Complexity and Emerging Technologies (formerly Net-Centric Computing Laboratory) and the College of Computer Studies will be holding the DLSU Hackercup 2015 this November 21-22, 2015. This is a 24-hour hackathon where students from DLSU and other academic insitutions will showcase their problem solving and technical and creative skills as they think and implement possible solutions to battle the crisis of poor data in our country.

We have partnered with the Open Data Philippines team to come up with a list of problems that they encounter in collecting, managing, presenting and utilizing data coming from different government agencies.

CATEGORIES
.
Commercial Applications
Commericial Apps

Commercial apps are intended to help average citizens with minimal to no prior knowledge of a given topic to understand and relate to the data. These apps center around a goal and uses open data for its content.

Utility
Applications
Utility Apps

Utility apps range from data collection to information publication. Currently, many government organizations are conducting surveys, cleaning data, summarizing data, and publishing data manually. There is a lso a need to expedite the process of manually making visual or text representations of a given set of data. Utility apps should aid personnel to perform these tasks easily.
